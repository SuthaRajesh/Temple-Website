Temple History

Old Temple History

KambaliNaicken Patti is a hamlet of Palakkanuthu Village, Dindigul District, Tamil Nadu. The hamlet was formed in 1928 after the railway line construction. According to Tamil tradition, “Kovil Illa Oorile Kudi Irukka Vendam” (No village should exist without a temple).

The villagers, mainly engaged in agriculture, raised a stone platform for Lord Ganesha under an Arasa Maram. Later, a mud shrine for Goddess Kaliamman was built with a small dome-shaped gopuram. A stone lamp post was also planted.

After India’s independence in 1947, villagers continued their worship and celebrated festivals. With growth in population and prosperity after 2014, they began planning for a larger temple to house both shrines together.

New Temple History

Finally on 18.8.2016 , an auspicious full moon day of month Avani, the People of Kambali Naicken Patti resolved in a village meeting to Construct a new temple for Lord Ganesha and Deity Kaliamman by collecting funds among the Village People. A savings Account was opened in the name A/M Vinayagar and Kaliamman temple. Fund collection work was started and the Villagers payed their amount wilfully to construct new temple. We cannot forget the donations paid by the daughters and son-in-laws of this village for the new temple.

From the funds collected, an agreement for the construction of this temple was made between the Oorgounder, fund managers and Stabadhi, Thiru. Vadivel , who is a temple architect studied at the MahabaliPuram Architecture College. The old deities were removed and shifted to temporary place during the Palalyam Ceremony on 26.10.2016.

New temple Vastu Poojai and Kudamuzhuku

Construction started with Vasthu Pooja, on 23.11.2016. Thus, the new stone temple for Lord Ganesha and Kaliamman completed in 2019.

The deities were placed in the new temple and Kudamuzhuku function was celebrated on 6.6.2019. The deities were energized on 48 day mandala Pooja . Till now, daily abisekam and arathana is conducted daily and the Lord and deity shower their blessings to the People of Kambali Naicken Patti.

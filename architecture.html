Temple Architecture

Structures

1. Front Mandapam: It is a rectangular shape 8 pillared concrete Mandapam. The pillars are structured with beautiful sculptures of Lord Muruga, Ganesha, their vahanas Pea cock, Moonjuru and also kamadenu, and Lion, the vahana of Deity Kali ma Devi.Front two pillars in east and west were attached with a Horse Warrior and soldier with sword underneath. Above the centre four pillars are 2 sets of Kulandai Balagars one with garland and another with hands in worshiping position. The bottom of the roof is with beautifully painted flowers continually. On either side of Ardhamandapam Entrance, large sized Dwara Shakthi stone statues are placed.

2. Ardha Mandapam: It is a square shaped completely stone constructed mandapam. At its entrance stone carved Devi Lakshmi with two elephants with garlands on each side is situated beautifully.

Towers

Raja Gopuram: Above Sanctum sanctorium the Rajagopuram is constructed with 5 stages with beautiful sculpures in all the four sides and painted well. On the top of this Rajagopuram placed 3 Kalasams with seeds during Kumbabishekam. Anti-lightning copper wire and Karungali wood apparatus were fixed during the construction to withstand any solar acute electrification.

Kannimoola Ganpathi Gopuram: On the south west corner of the elevated site of the temple, a small 3 staged gopuram is constructed for Kanni moola Ganapathi.

Karpaga Ganapathi Gopuram: Outside the elevated site of the temple, in the North-East side also a small gopuram with 3 stages is constructed for Karpaga Ganapathi. This could facilitate the devotees to perform Jalabisekam to Ganesha at any time they decide. There is also a cooling sheet shed before this gopuram and an elevated praharam surrounding the gopuram for the benefit of devotees to perform their rituals during hot sun and rains.

Halls and Sculptures

There is a madapalli room outside the temple site to cook pongals and prepare prasadams or Annadhanas during festivals and ritual days. There is a Kalachara Mandapam constructed by the Village Panchayat east of Madapalli, which is used by the Village Public to perform cultural Programmes during festivals like Pongal, annual Kali festivals, Pillyar sathurthi, Adi velli and so.

Shrines and Deities

The Main Ma Kali Vigraga is made up of Stone and placed at the sanctum above the stone pedam. Opposite to the Kali Vigraham, Simha Vahanam and Palipedam is placed in the Mahamandapam. There is also two gigantic stone shrines of Dwara Shakthi are placed in the Mahamandapam just before the entrance of ArdhaMandapam. Stone shrine of Lord Ganesa with Moonjuru vahanam and Pali pedam are placed in Kanni Moola Ganapathi temple and Karpaga Ganapathi Temple. In front of the elevated temple site in line with main Ma kali deity, placed a stone pillar as Deepa Stambam in which oil lamp be lighted during Karthigai Deepam festival. On the western side of the Deepa stambam, 2 sculptures of Oor Velappar, the village patrolling deities are placed. On the four corners of the fence of elevated temple site, four shrines of Muneeswaran in placed as Kaval Deivams whom believed to be the killers of evils.

Kumbabishekam

After construction of new temple in the present site, Kumbabisekam festivel was celebrated amid heavy showers of rain for the past several years, on 06.06.2019 by the Village Public.
